Off-Road Vehicle Brake Pedal

Relevant Skills

  • SolidWorks
  • SolidWorks Simulation
  • Waterjet Cutter and Lathe Operation

Why?

I was a member of the University of British Columbia’s Baja SAE student design team, where we designed and manufactured off-road vehicles for competition. I was tasked to design a brake pedal that was stronger and lighter than the previous year’s pedal, which yielded under heavy use.

About the Project

The project required me to apply in-class knowledge to the design of the part. Concepts like stress concentration and yield criterion from solid mechanics and fatigue lifetime from materials science proved to be extremely useful while iterating on the pedal’s design.

I modeled the components in SolidWorks and evaluated strength properties through finite element analysis in SolidWorks Simulation. Additionally, the team uses SolidWorks PDM, so I was able to familiarize myself with the data management system in a collaborative work environment.

My brake pedal redesign was 25% lighter and had a factor of safety three times that of the previous year’s design.

Special consideration was made to ensure compatibility with the rest of the vehicle. This required continuous conversation and weekly meetings with the chassis team.

I ended the project by presenting my design to the team in a formal design review. Fabricating the part allowed me to develop waterjet and machining skills.
