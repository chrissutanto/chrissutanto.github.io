Laptop Stand

Relevant Skills

  • CNC Machining and Toolpath Planning
  • SolidWorks
  • Fusion 360

Why?

I found the need to elevate my laptop to a more comfortable viewing height. I could’ve stacked a couple textbooks and call it a day, but I wanted something that looked neat. I’ve always found something appealing about Ikea’s design style, so I tried to make something that would feel at home on their shelves.

About the Project

Matching Ikea's general product aesthetic was not my only concern. To fit within their product line, my laptop stand had to match the utility of their flat-pack furniture system. Disassembled, the stand takes up roughly the same volume as a 13” laptop. Assembly is simple and toolless—no Allen wrench required.

I designed the stand in SolidWorks and prepared the toolpaths in Fusion 360. Parts were cut on my home-built CNC router (MPCNC) out of less than a square foot of 3/4” common board. The connecting rods were made from 5/8” wood dowel. I 3D printed my favorite part: the white inlays.
