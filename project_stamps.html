Why?

After a summer working at the Kaushansky Lab at the Center for Global Infectious Disease Research, I looked for a parting gift to give to everyone in the lab. The lab studies malaria parasites and operates one of the only mosquito insectaries in the US, so I knew the gift had to be mosquito related.

About the Project

I decided to make mosquito stamps as I had an interest in linocut printmaking at the time. Unfortunately, I didnâ€™t have the artistic ability or the patience to hand carve a dozen and a half stamps for my coworkers. Fortunately, I had recently put together a CNC router.

Right as I got home after my second to last day of work, I glued a sheet of linoleum onto a board of wood and clamped it down. I opened up Inkscape and sketched up a (roughly anatomically correct) mosquito design. After importing the vector image into Fusion 360 and preparing the toolpath, I loaded it onto the CNC router and started to cut. It was a pretty late night for me, but I was happy with the result.

The mosquito stamp was the first of a few experiments with CNC stamp making, another being a monstera deliciosa leaf stamp made fully out of wood. As the linoleum mosquito stamp was analogous to linocut printmaking, I made the monstera leaf stamp without linoleum in an attempt to recreate woodblock printing.

I've also made a couple signs using the same process, one being a "Louisiana" sign for my little sister's presentation on the state.
