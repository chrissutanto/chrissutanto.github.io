3D Printed Colostomy Bag Attachment System

Relevant Skills

  • SolidWorks
  • Design for 3D Printing

Why?

To collect waste, colostomy bags are typically connected by an adhesive to an opening in the abdomen. The use of low-quality adhesives, which is common in less developed countries, can result in life-threatening complications. My grandfather saw this issue in his home country of Indonesia and designed an adhesive-free system.

He has personally used his original design for the past few decades. To help him share his idea with others, I recreated the system in SolidWorks, updated its design, and optimized it for 3D printing.

About the Project

As I can’t personally use the device, I communicated with my grandfather to understand a user’s perspective. This allowed me to understand and iterate on his original design.

I experimented with print settings and materials until I had a system that was consistent and easily reproducible. These settings were documented and sent over along with the 3D models to a hospital in Indonesia, where they will begin to produce them on-demand for patients.
